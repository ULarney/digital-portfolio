## Reflection: CV Creation (STAR Method)

This reflection outlines the process of building my CV using the **STAR** framework.

### Situation
As an ICT in Application Development student at CPUT, I needed a professional CV that clearly presents my technical skills, work experience, and academic background. The CV would serve as a key asset for internship applications, job opportunities, and my GitHub portfolio.

### Task
My main objective was to create a one‑page CV that:
- Highlights my programming, web development, and database management skills.
- Showcases my IT support experience in a way that demonstrates problem‑solving and customer‑centric service.
- Presents my education and personal profile with a professional tone.
- Follows a clean, scannable layout suitable for both human reviewers and applicant tracking systems.

### Action
I took the following steps:
1. **Content inventory** – listed all experiences and extracted key achievements.
2. **Structure & layout** – used a reverse‑chronological layout with clear sections.
3. **Writing with STAR** – for work experience bullet points, I implicitly applied the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to highlight problem‑solving and impact.
4. **Refinement** – reviewed for grammar, consistency, and impact.

### Result
The completed CV successfully presents a cohesive narrative of my technical growth. It has received positive feedback for clarity and professionalism, and now serves as a cornerstone of my GitHub portfolio demonstrating both my technical abilities and my attention to detail.
